Diplomacy

Australia and Japan Sign Sweeping New Pact on Defence, Energy and Critical Minerals

Albanese and Takaichi unveil agreement designed to shore up regional security and economic ties amid a turbulent Indo-Pacific.

Australia and Japan have struck a wide-ranging new agreement to deepen security and economic ties, jointly announced this week by Prime Minister Anthony Albanese and his Japanese counterpart Sanae Takaichi. The pact spans defence cooperation, energy security, critical minerals supply chains and broader economic resilience.

Officials in both capitals framed the deal as a response to a more contested regional environment and to growing demand for stable, democratic supply partners in advanced manufacturing inputs. The critical minerals chapter is expected to dovetail with Australia's existing offtake arrangements with allied partners.

The economic security provisions cover joint resilience planning for energy and food, while the defence track formalises closer interoperability between the Australian Defence Force and Japan's Self-Defense Forces. A ministerial review is scheduled within twelve months.

Business · NDIS
Reform

“Securing the NDIS” bill returns to Parliament with budget

Canberra correspondent

The federal government has confirmed it will introduce the National Disability Insurance Scheme Amendment (Securing the NDIS for Future Generations) Bill alongside the 2026–27 Budget. The package proposes to remove around 160,000 participants from the scheme over four years and to reduce average plan funding by approximately A$5,000 within two years.

Eligibility will move away from medical-diagnosis gating toward an assessment of functional capacity, environment and individual aspirations. Claims and payments systems will be progressively rebuilt from July 2026, with a full rollout targeted by the end of 2030.

Central Care Regulatory Impact

The shift to functional-capacity assessments and tighter eligibility will reshape the participant pipeline that Central Care services. A meaningful share of new referrals is likely to be either rejected or routed onto smaller plans, while existing participants face plan reductions averaging A$5,000 — directly squeezing the volume of support coordination and plan management hours per file.

Action: Stress-test FY27 revenue against three scenarios — flat, –10%, –20% participant volume — and prepare client communications explaining the new functional-capacity framework before assessments begin.

Plan Management

Plan management to be commissioned through panel from October 2027

The federal government has confirmed it will commission a panel of plan management providers, with implementation from 1 October 2027 following a six-month transition. The objective, according to officials, is to lift quality and integrity standards and reduce fraud, while addressing pricing across plan management, support coordination and home-and-living supports.

Central Care Strategic Risk & Opportunity

A commissioned panel model means plan management will become a contracted market — not an open one. Providers that aren't on the panel risk being excluded; providers that are on it will see steadier flow but tighter pricing and integrity scrutiny. Quality and audit posture become commercial differentiators, not back-office hygiene.

Action: Begin panel-readiness work now: documented quality systems, fraud controls, audited financials, and a clear value proposition for participants and the agency.

Support Coordination

New support coordination function to begin July 2028

A newly commissioned support coordination function will commence from 1 July 2028, with the NDIS Commission's earlier work on mandatory registration for support coordinators having been paused in December 2025. Budgets for social and community participation supports are due to begin changing from 1 October 2026, with the broader new planning framework rolling out from mid-2026 through to 2030.

Central Care Growth Opportunity

The two-and-a-half-year window before the commissioned support coordination function begins is a build window, not a wait window. Operators that arrive in July 2028 with deep participant relationships, clean outcome data, and integrated plan management will be best placed to win commissioned slots.

Action: Capture and report participant outcomes consistently from now through 2028 — that data will be the strongest commissioning evidence.

2026 tipped as the turning point for embedded finance in Australia

Fintech desk

Industry analysts are calling 2026 the year embedded financial services move from “optional” to “essential” for growth-oriented Australian platforms. B2B embedded payments globally are projected to reach US$2.6 trillion in 2026, with embedded invoice finance among the fastest-moving categories. Australia's open banking and Consumer Data Right frameworks are cited as enabling conditions, alongside maturing platform distribution.

PaidNow Growth Opportunity

The macro thesis underpinning PaidNow is now squarely consensus — embedded invoice finance is moving from edge case to default expectation on B2B platforms. The implication is that distribution windows with the largest trades and strata platforms will close faster than expected, and competitor platforms will start bundling similar offerings.

Action: Accelerate the SMATA partnership rollout and lock in two more platform partnerships in the next quarter while the category is still being defined.

Strata

NSW strata reforms take effect, lifting accountability for developers and managers

The latest tranche of NSW strata reforms began operating from 1 April 2026, improving repairs and maintenance planning, strengthening developer accountability, and offering better support to incoming buyers. Adoption of digital maintenance platforms, integrated levy and expense systems, and smart-building telemetry continues to accelerate, with operators warning that schemes slow to digitise risk falling behind on cost and compliance.

TradesNow Regulatory Tailwind

Stronger repair-and-maintenance planning obligations push strata managers toward systems that can produce auditable job records, quotes, scheduling and invoicing. That is precisely the surface area TradesNow already covers — and the SMATA-aligned distribution channel into 3,000 strata managers becomes more valuable in a regime where compliance evidence sits with the strata manager.

Action: Package a “NSW reform compliance” template inside TradesNow — quote, schedule, invoice, payment evidence — and pitch it through SMATA in the next 30 days.

Construction

Australian construction market to grow 6.9% in 2026 even as labour shortages persist

Forecasters expect Australia's construction market to expand by 6.9% in 2026 to roughly A$193 billion, driven by AUKUS-linked defence investment, data centre construction and metro megaprojects. Hybrid teams pairing skilled workers with automated systems, drones for site inspections, BIM and digital twins are now baseline tools, even as labour shortages, rising input costs and cash-flow stress weigh on smaller builders.

PaidNow · TradesNow Demand Signal

Cash-flow stress at the bottom of the contractor stack is the single most cited pain point in this cycle. That is the exact problem PaidNow solves for subcontractors, and TradesNow's job management layer is the data trail that makes invoice finance underwritable. Rising volumes plus tighter margins is the textbook backdrop for embedded payment products.

Action: Sharpen the joint pitch — TradesNow as the system of record, PaidNow as instant payment — and target sub-contractor segments most exposed to cash-flow stress (electrical, plumbing, fit-out).
