Iran Closes Strait of Hormuz, Threatens to Attack Ships

Strait of Hormuz — satellite imagery

An Iranian military commander declared the Strait of Hormuz closed to all maritime traffic, threatening to set fire to any vessel attempting to cross the waterway. The strait, which handles roughly one-fifth of the world’s total oil supply, is now at the centre of the most significant naval standoff since the 1980s Tanker War.

International shipping companies have begun rerouting tankers via the longer Cape of Good Hope route, adding two weeks and millions of dollars in transit costs. Oil futures surged eight per cent on the announcement.

CNN, Reuters

Trump Pauses US Strikes on Iran’s Energy Infrastructure

President Trump announced a 10-day pause in strikes against Iran’s energy sites, extending until April 6. More than 1,750 people have been killed in Iran since US-Israel strikes began on February 28. The pause comes amid intense back-channel diplomacy, with Qatar mediating between Washington and Tehran.

CNN

Australia Bans Iranian Visitors Amid Tensions

NDIS Framework Planning Rollout Begins Mid-2026

The Australian government is introducing a new framework planning model for the NDIS starting mid-2026, beginning with a small number of participants before a broader rollout. New SIL and platform provider registration requirements take effect from July 1, requiring all existing providers to re-register under updated categories.

The structured support needs assessments will fundamentally change how participant budgets are allocated, with the government projecting annual savings of $2.3 billion once fully implemented.

NDIS Official, The Guardian

Central Care Regulatory Impact

New provider registration requirements from July 1 directly affect Central Care as a registered NDIS provider. The structured support needs assessments will change how participant budgets are allocated, which could shift spending patterns for on-demand services.

Action: Audit Central Care’s provider registration against the new July 1 categories and begin any re-registration process early.

$10 Billion Foundational Supports Program Committed

Federal and state governments have committed $10 billion over five years to establish “foundational supports” for people with disabilities who do not meet NDIS eligibility. The program covers early intervention and community-based services, with a staged rollout reaching full national coverage by 2028.

Department of Health, Disability and Ageing

Central Care Growth Opportunity

This $10B program creates a new market of people with disabilities who don’t qualify for NDIS but will now receive funded support. Central Care’s on-demand model is well-suited to serve this segment as state governments build delivery infrastructure.

NDIS Fraud Probe Uncovers Potential $5B in Losses

Trades Workforce Shortage Peaks: 126,000 Gap Projected by Mid-2027

Every construction-specific occupation in Australia is now listed as in national shortage — the only industry in which every role faces universal shortages. The gap for trades workers and labourers is projected to peak at 126,000 by mid-2027, with electricians, plumbers and carpenters the hardest to fill.

Jobs and Skills Australia

TradesNow Market Tailwind

Universal trade shortages make efficient job matching more critical than ever. Strata managers struggling to find reliable trades will be more receptive to a curated B2B marketplace. TradesNow’s position inside SMATA’s 3,000 strata manager network means it can aggregate demand and make the existing 15,000 registered trades more accessible.

NSW Strata Law Reforms Take Effect April 1

Major strata reforms hit New South Wales from April 1, including mandatory maintenance schedules for new developments, independent surveyor certification, and new 10-year capital works fund plan requirements. The reforms affect more than 1.2 million strata lots across the state.

NSW Government, Strata Community Association

TradesNow Direct Impact

Mandatory maintenance schedules and 10-year capital works plans mean strata managers will need to procure more trades work, more consistently, and with better documentation. This directly increases demand for a structured B2B marketplace.

Action: Position TradesNow’s launch messaging around helping strata managers meet the new April 1 NSW compliance requirements.
